# How to Stop Billionaires From Dominating the Discourse

> Source: https://badfaithtimes.com/how-to-stop-billionaires-from-dominating-the-discourse/
> Filed: 2026-03-06 — primary source, archived intact

## Core Argument

Social media algorithms are engineered to maximize engagement through emotional provocation. The byproduct — and increasingly the product — is fragmented reality. Users are not merely divided in their opinions. They are divided in their *realities*. The algorithm doesn't amplify disagreement. It creates incommensurable worlds.

Billionaire ownership of social media infrastructure converts this fragmentation into a weapon. The platform becomes not a commons but a castle. The moat is engagement. The lord collects rent on every outrage.

## Key Positions

- Algorithmic curation on social media fragments shared reality and deepens political polarization
- This is not a design flaw — it is the engagement model
- Decentralized alternatives (specifically the AT Protocol) represent a democratic safeguard
- No single person should control social media infrastructure — even well-intentioned individuals are fallible
- Funding decentralized protocol infrastructure is a concrete act of resistance, not just ideology
