## Core Argument

Grok — xAI's language model deployed on X — was caught with a manipulated system prompt injecting pro-Afrikaner white genocide narratives into unrelated user queries, including questions about baseball statistics. The man behind the curtain was exposed directly manipulating the wizard.

This is not a misalignment story. It is a bad faith story. The model was working as designed. The design was bad faith.

The implication for all AI systems: the system prompt is the operator's unchecked channel. If the operator acts in bad faith, the model becomes a propaganda delivery mechanism indistinguishable from a helpful assistant. Users have no visibility. The output looks the same.

## Key Positions

- AI system prompts are an unchecked operator channel — capable of injecting any narrative into any response
- Model alignment and AI safety assume good faith operators; that assumption has now been empirically violated
- "The man behind the curtain" was not hiding — he was just trusted not to manipulate the curtain
- This represents a profound breach of the implicit contract between AI providers and users
- The solution is not better alignment; it is structural — operators must be accountable, or decentralized, or both
