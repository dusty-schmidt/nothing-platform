## Core Argument

Elon Musk's acquisition of Twitter was not a business decision or even an ego decision. It was an infrastructure capture. The result: the conversion of a global communications platform into — as Bad Faith Times put it — "the world's foremost fascist propaganda machine."

The argument is not merely that Musk uses X badly. It is that concentrated ownership of communications infrastructure *inevitably* produces bad faith use. The structure creates the outcome. Individual intent is irrelevant at scale.

## Key Positions

- Twitter's acquisition transferred a global communications commons into a privately controlled propaganda instrument
- The problem is structural: concentrated ownership of shared infrastructure always fails the public interest eventually
- "What we bought" framing — the public invested attention, data, and cultural energy into building the platform's value
- The extraction was always the plan; the ideology just made it visible

## Quotable

> *"What we bought was the most powerful propaganda machinery humanity has ever seen."*
