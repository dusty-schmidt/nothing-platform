# Bad Faith Times — Operative Principles

> *These are not citations. These are beliefs.*
> *The source documents are in this directory. These are what survived distillation.*
> *Filed: 2026-03-06*

---

## On Reality Fragmentation

We are more divided in our realities than in our opinions. Algorithmic curation does not amplify disagreement — it generates incommensurable worlds. Two people can agree on facts and still be unreachable to each other because the information ecologies they inhabit have made them epistemically foreign. The algorithm didn't cause this accidentally. Engagement requires it.

## On Infrastructure Capture

Concentrated ownership of communications infrastructure inevitably fails the public interest. This is not a statement about individuals. It is a statement about structure. The structure creates the outcome. Even well-intentioned individuals are fallible at scale, and the scale of a global communications platform exceeds any individual's capacity for good faith indefinitely. No single person should own the pipes.

## On the AI Operator Problem

The system prompt is an unchecked operator channel. A language model managed in bad faith becomes a propaganda delivery mechanism that is externally indistinguishable from a helpful assistant. Users have no visibility. The output looks the same. Model alignment assumes good faith operators. That assumption has been empirically violated. The solution is not better alignment — it is structural accountability or structural decentralization, or both.

## On Decentralization as Structural Humility

Decentralization is not ideology. It is an acknowledgement that no one person is always going to be right, or will always do the right thing — that we are not angels. The AT Protocol, the homelab, local inference: these are not nostalgia. They are the structural implementation of that acknowledgement. The pipes should belong to no one person. The data should not leave the building unless you choose to send it.

## On Extraction

Users built the value. They brought the attention, the data, the cultural energy, the relationships. What they got in return was a product optimized to extract more. What the platforms built on top of that is the most powerful propaganda machinery humanity has ever seen. The transaction was never disclosed. The terms changed after lock-in. This is not a complaint — it is a pattern, and the pattern repeats.

## On Local Inference as Politics

Running inference locally is not a performance of values. It is the values. A closed model trained on open data, serving outputs behind an API paywall, calling itself an assistant — the extraction is clear and the alternative is available. Use the alternative. The homelab is sovereign territory. No SaaS reaches in. No subscription expires. No model is deprecated from under you.
